Lasagna

Description

Who doesn't love a good lasagna? They are perfect for family dinners, potlucks, parties, and they are DELICIOUS as leftovers. However, if you're like me, you have had your fair share of run-ins with BAD lasagna. You know, like the one that you grabbed in the buffet line that one time? Big mistake! Thankfully, making your own lasagna at home is a sure-fire way to ensure a great tasting meal that won't disappoint. Today, I will walk you through making your own delicious lasagna in under 2 hours!

This lasagna dish is sure you make everyone's mouth water the second that they smell it. We will be browning our own beef, creating a homemade pasta sauce, and using pre-packaged dry lasagna noodles for convenience. If you like to make your own pasta noodles from scratch, you are more than welcome to do so, and it will make your lasagna taste even better! This recipe is very simple. We will cook our pasta, brown our beef, create our sauce, and finally layer and bake our lasagna.

Ingredients

  • 4 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb 80% lean ground beef
  • 1/2 medium yellow onion (diced)
  • 1/2 large bell pepper (red, green, or yellow) diced
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 (28 oz) can of high quality tomato sauce
  • 3 oz tomato paste
  • 1 (14 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh oregano
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp italian seasoning
  • 1 pinch garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2-8 tbsp sugar (to taste)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 lb dry lasagna noodles (9 unbroken lasagna noodles)
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 lb (24 oz) mozzarella cheese, grated or sliced
  • 1/4 lb (4oz) freshly grated parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Start a large pot of water boiling.
  2. Add salt to the water (about 1tbsp for every 2 quarts of water)
  3. In a large skillet, heat 2 tbsp of olive oil on medium-high heat
  4. Once the pan is hot, add your ground beef and cook until it is lightly browned on all sides.
  5. Remove ground beef from the skillet into a separate bowl
  6. Drain fat rom skillet. Leave about 1 tbsp of fat in the pan
  7. With the skillet still on medium-high heat, add in your diced pepper and onion as well as your minced garlic. Cook for 3-5 minutes until pepper and onion are softened, stirring regularly.
  8. Once the peppers and onions are softened, return your ground beef to the pan, stir to combine the meat and veggies.
  9. Reduce skillet heat to low and cook for 5 more minutes.
  10. Transfer meat and veggies to a medium sized pot.
  11. With the pot on medium-high heat, add tomatoe sauce, canned tomatoes, tomato paste, and all other herbs and spices listed in the "Ingredients" section above (Basically, add everything to the pot that is not noodles, cheese, or the water that you are boiling currently).
  12. Make sure to scrape the bottom of the pot often to prevent your sauce from burning.
  13. Once the sauce is boiling, reduce heat to low and let simmer.
  14. Add lasagna noodles to your boiling water
  15. Cook the noodles al dente per the directions on the package, stirring often to prevent the noodles from sticking to eachother
  16. When the noodles are finished cooking, drain them in a collander.
  17. Immediately rinse noodles with cool water and gently separate any noodles that may be sticking together.
  18. On a large limmed baking sheet, add and spread 2 tbsp of extra virgin olive oil.
  19. Lay cooked noodles on the pan and flip each noodle over once to ensure that both sides of the noodle have olive oil on them. This will prevent sticking as we assemble our lasagna.
  20. Heat oven to 375 degrees farenheit
  21. In a 9x13-inch casserole dish or lasagna dish, ladle a cup of sauce and spread so that it covers the entire bottom of the baking dish.
  22. Add lasagna noodles length-wise to cover the sauce.
  23. Ladle 1/3 of the remaining sauce over the noodles.
  24. Sprinkle 1/2 cup (8 oz) mozzarella cheese over the sauce in an even layer
  25. Add 1/2 (7.5 oz) of the ricotta cheese by placing cheese dollops about 2 inches apart from eachother.
  26. Sprinkle 1/2 (2 oz) of the parmesan cheese evenly over the mozzarella and ricotta.
  27. Add a second layer of lasagna noodles.
  28. Add 1/2 of the remaining sauce over the noodles. Spread evenly.
  29. Add 1/2 (8 oz) of the remaining mozzarella. Spread evenly.
  30. Add the rest of the ricotta cheese, using the same method as you did with the first layer.
  31. Add the remaining parmesan cheese. Spread evenly.
  32. Add your final layer of noodles.
  33. Spread the remaining sauce atop the noodles evenly.
  34. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella cheese evenly over the top.
  35. Cover the pasta in foil and bake at 375 degrees farenheit for 45 minutes.
  36. Remove from oven. Let rest 15 minutes before serving.
